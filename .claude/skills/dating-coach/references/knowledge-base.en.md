## 0. Underlying Logic (the foundation beneath every technique)

- **Value + availability = Attraction.** Value alone isn't enough—she has to feel you're "within reach"; but there can't be zero barrier to entry either.
- **Not exposing neediness** is the first principle; neediness is the biggest killer of attraction. Distinguish "wanting" from "neediness"—you're allowed to want, but you can't be anxious, clingy, or at her beck and call.
- **Women admire strength; the pursuer is worth less than the chooser.** Put yourself in the position of being chosen, not perpetually chasing and currying favor.
- **She chats with you every day because she likes you; she won't come to like you because you chat with her every day.** Don't burn through her patience with sheer chat frequency.
- **Cultivating yourself > doggedly chasing one person.** She draws close to you because you're in good shape and have an overflow of good things to offer. "Rather than chase the horse, plant the grass; when the flowers bloom, the butterflies come on their own."
- **Quantity changes quality.** Too few options → you treat your one target as your whole world → you become anxious about gains and losses and lower your standards. Expand your social circle.
- **A sense of deservingness** replaces the transactional "give-and-take" mindset.

## 1. Meeting People / Approaching

- **Expand your social circle:** interest communities, sports, friends' gatherings, coworkers and classmates, dating apps. Opportunities come from sheer numbers.
- **First impression = image + state** (see Section 6). First get your hardware and mental state up.
- **Breaking the ice:** be the first to dissolve the strangeness; subconsciously don't treat her as a stranger. An opener can be a single expression/emoji or one relaxed line—no formal "Hello," and don't start interrogating her right away. Entry points: comment on her looks ("cutie/big eyes"), find a topic from her Moments or the current situation, or describe your own state to create a sense of familiarity.
- **Approaching mindset:** high-status posture, not creepy, no big deal if rejected. If you stir an emotional ripple, it counts as a success.
- **Getting contact info:** ask naturally when the interaction is back-and-forth, when you've made her laugh, or when she's actively picking up the conversation; you can use a "time bridge" to plant the expectation of meeting again ("My friends have a get-together this weekend—come hang out with us").

## 2. Chatting

### Overall Principles
- **The core of chatting is transmitting emotion**; without emotion you're just draining her patience. "A year of ineffective chatting is worth less than a day of effective chatting."
- **Male thinking solves problems; female thinking wants emotional resonance.** When she vents, empathize first—don't rush to hand her a solution.
- Rhythm formula: **talk about her → talk about me → talk about us.**
- **Don't ask more than 2–3 questions in a row** (it becomes an interrogation). The fix: alternate sharing about yourself with asking about her.
- Chatting is like boxing—you throw punches and you pull them back; **leave on a high note**, ending on your own initiative at the hottest point so she's left wanting more.
- **Mystery/intrigue = make her guess:** control frequency, end conversations first, match her reply speed and message length, "the busier you are, the more attractive."

### Push-Pull / Flirting Techniques
- **Push-pull:** praise laced with a jab—affirm a strength first, then lightly knock a flaw; never let the force become a personal attack.
- **Dual-track:** the literal line (serious info) crossed with the playful line (teasing humor); pure literal is dull.
- **Playful misinterpretation:** twist her words into "she's chasing me / taking advantage of me," flipping the roles.
- **Assumptive close:** skip the asking and act as if she's already agreed (don't say "what day are you free," say "what day should I take you to that place to eat").
- **Negging > putting down:** negging is milder, more nonchalant, and actually lands harder; putting someone down looks deliberate and is very low.
- **Diverting attention:** drop a more sensitive word into your escalation line so she overlooks what you're actually doing.
- **Stating intent / planting expectation:** describe a future intimate scene—this tests the window and stirs an emotional ripple.
- **Self-protection against rejection:** if you predict she'll reject you, say it for her in advance ("I'm so handsome you wouldn't dare like me")—it's the same rejection, but coming from your mouth it becomes humor.
- **Cut your losses / cut the topic:** when her responses get worse, don't keep fighting; cutting the topic on your own both limits losses and lowers neediness.

### All-Purpose Topics (high extensibility, low risk)
1. **Travel** (the most useful): talk about specific places + your own take. Four elements: culture (shows the most class—have your own viewpoint, even some good-natured griping, but avoid stereotypes), scenery (point out "great for photos"), food (take an unconventional angle—talk about obscure spots, leverage information gaps), and climate. You don't have to have actually been there to talk about a place.
2. **Film & TV:** don't discuss plot themes—**talk about the stars / recent hits**; read your audience.
3. **Wellness** (the most opportunities): when she's sick, don't say "drink more water"—teach her to **relieve the immediate discomfort** first, then advise her to see a doctor; specialize in period cramps (only after she brings it up herself) and insomnia (offer "interesting" little tricks); for weight loss, don't push "500 jump-ropes a day" or diet pills.
4. **Astrology / MBTI:** an opening for cold reading.

### Cold Reading (make her feel "you get me")
- The essence: exploit a cognitive gap—people only pay attention to themselves and overlook that "everyone else is the same." Formula = analysis + implication.
- The calibration: create the feeling of "I really understand you"—you must **never** create the fear of "I've seen right through you."
- Templates you can use: "On the surface you're carefree, but deep down there's a little girl who's more anxious than anyone." "You look like you have lots of friends, but only two or three you truly open up to." "You trust people too easily and care too much about their response, so you often end up let down."

## 3. Female Psychology: Reading the Signals

> **No single signal should be read in isolation—cross-verify multiple signals**; the core response is always "acknowledge the emotion/fact first, then guide her to express more."

- **Interested questions she asks** (her questions = emotional investment): she asks about your weekend plans, your dating values, your social circle, "would it annoy you if I vented to you," your past relationships, your future plans—respectively she's looking for chances to spend time together, confirming your values, testing trust, testing whether you can hold her emotions, checking for hidden problems, and gauging your sense of responsibility.
- **Overt signs she genuinely likes you** (≥2 means ~70% interest; ≥4 means you can move things forward): she chats with no purpose just to chat, remembers niche details, shows vulnerability, weaves you into her social activity, allows physical escalation, plans the future with you on her own, drops her guard (shows up bare-faced), and shares negative emotions while seeking comfort.
- **Physical signals** (judge holistically, factoring in body orientation): body squared toward you, glancing back shyly after eye contact, playing with her hair, toes pointed at you, allowing close proximity, mirroring your movements, genuine laughter (eyes crinkling), not flinching when touched and even touching back = interest; turning sideways or away, playing on her phone, arms crossed, deliberately keeping distance, stiff dodging = no interest.
- **Covert rejection** (≥3 means cut your losses): consistently perfunctory replies, declining every invite, not sharing her private life, deliberately keeping distance, ignoring your future plans, frequently dismissing your strengths.

## 4. Frames and Shit Tests

### Frame
- **Frame = the implicit positioning conveyed during the interaction (who leads, who is high-value) = control of the conversation.**
- **A strong frame ≠ a hard-line attitude** (the most common misreading). Overdoing the hard line just makes you a "small man with a big temper." A frame needs **flexibility**.
- Common techniques: lob the question back (she asks "what's your type" → "what do you think my type is"—don't answer head-on and hand over the initiative); cover her with a bigger frame; flip the stance when you're at a disadvantage ("you wouldn't happen to be taking advantage and then bolting, would you?"); make the decision for her when she's hesitating (lead step by step, not by striking a hard-line attitude from the outset).

### Handling Shit Tests
**Four principles:** give no interest indicator / don't get emotional / don't launch into long explanations / recognize it's unconscious and is a double-edged sword.

**Four universal formulas:**
1. "You said the same thing in your past life" → steer toward sensual flirtation.
2. Lob the question back → ("Do you flirt with girls a lot?" → "Do you feel like I'm flirting with you?").
3. "Should I pretend to be a little hurt?" → demonstrates a strong frame (response to "you're not my type").
4. Seize the high ground → call her out ("Having fun being cheeky there?").

**Advanced (respond with concrete facts):**
- "Are you like this with every girl?" → "Depends on the person—I only put in extra effort with ones I click with. I chat with you more than the others, don't I?"
- "You're a bit worse than my ex." → "He suited the old you; maybe I suit the you of now better."
- "I don't want to date right now." → "Friends is good too—if you want to date later, it won't be too late to consider it then." (no pressure)
- "Will you still like me if I get fat?" → "What I like is your humor and warmth, and those won't change; if you really gain weight, we'll work out together."

## 5. Dating (In Person)

- **Location:** for a first date, avoid the traps of a solo KTV/internet café/sweat-inducing hot pot or BBQ. Prioritize **Type-A locations** (built-in activity, no need to force conversation: ice rink = built-in hand-holding, pet shop = sparks her maternal instinct, escape room/arcade/amusement park), then use **Type-B locations** (café/quiet bar/park/restaurant) to elevate things through conversation. Wind down at a quiet bar to get pleasantly tipsy (not drunk).
- **Flow:** for a first date, block out a big chunk of time (3–7 hours), **change venues at least 3 times** (creating the sense of "we did a lot"), and **mix active and quiet** (active settings deepen impressions more easily and bail out guys who aren't good at chatting).
- **Picking up the tab:** reality leans toward the man taking the initiative, within your means; fixating on the money actually exposes you and costs you points.
- **When she brings her close friend:** usually it means she doesn't want to advance things today. Last-minute notice that she's bringing her → politely decline; told in advance but the relationship is only so-so → politely decline; you've been chatting really well and she tells you in advance → graciously let her bring the friend, use the "70-30 rule" (give more attention to the friend to make her jealous) + "bribe the friend to put in a good word for you."
- **Escalating physical touch** (natural + withdraw promptly): on the street, guide her to the inner side with a hand on her shoulder, then let go → seated side by side, lightly touch on the pretext of sharing a video and watch her reaction → crossing the street, first take her by the wrist (not the palm) and release once you've crossed; next time don't release—naturally slide into interlaced fingers → at night in a quiet spot, an embrace; if eye contact holds for over 2 seconds, lean in first, and when you're nearly there, stop and let her complete the final move. **If you only eat, drink, and play without any touch, her interest won't grow.**
- **Wrapping up:** at the tail end, set up the next date in person (in person she's more likely to agree); space the second invite at least a day out, and when setting a time don't pester for a specific day—just ask "what day works for you?"; stay in touch without going silent, but don't grovel.

## 6. Image / Display (the hardware threshold)

- **Five outfit styles:** casual sporty / Japanese workwear / Korean boyfriend / business power-suit / refined rogue. Six principles: no more than three colors; bridge colors with a same-color base layer; busy on top, simple on the bottom; accent with accessories; same color family looks classier; echo two items in the same color.
- **Hairstyles:** fade buzz cut (manly, gives a sense of security), Korean textured fringe, retro slick-back, comma hair, top knot—choose by face shape/vibe.
- **Personal hygiene (very important):** trim nails and nose hair, no stubble, always use mouthwash/gum before a date. Point-losers: long nails, dirty shoes, pilling clothes, bad breath.
- **Building your Moments (social feed)** (your "dating résumé"): four kinds of display—looks / quality of life / availability (hinting you're single) / preselection (women around you but no flirtation). Principles: rely on "hiding" not "showing off," few but fine (1–2 photos), clean it up regularly; you can use "visible to her only" to deepen the impression without exposing neediness; comment rather than like to test the window.
- **High-energy state:** energy transmits (you put out 100, she receives 50). A short workout before a date can fire up your emotions, but don't drain yourself.
- **Attitude foundation:** equality, self-respect (zero tolerance for being stood up), no neediness, providing emotional value.

## 7. Making It Official and Beyond

> See lifecycle.en.md for details. Key point: confessing your feelings should come naturally once attraction is sufficient (flirtation is well-developed, physical escalation flows smoothly, she gives you a sense of exclusivity)—not using a confession to "beg" for the relationship; very often a natural mutual confirmation beats a formal confession. After that comes long-term coexistence, emotional maintenance, conflict handling, meeting the parents, and moving toward marriage—what carries you there is no longer lines and tactics, but sincerity, responsibility, shared growth, and the ability to handle real-world problems.
