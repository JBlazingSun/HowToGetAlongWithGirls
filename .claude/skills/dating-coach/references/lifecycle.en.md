# Full-Lifecycle Roadmap

> 🌐 **Language**: English (current) · [中文 / Chinese](./lifecycle.md)

For use with "Flow C: Stage Diagnosis." When the user asks "How should I move things forward?", "What stage are we at?", or "What's my next step?", first **pinpoint which stage he's in**, then give the task for that stage and the next action.

## Stage Overview

```
Stage 0: Self-Development (the foundation running through everything)
│
Getting-to-Know phase (the pursuit period, split into four sub-stages):
   1. Attraction  →  2. Plateau  →  3. Flirtation  →  4. Relationship
│
5. Long-Term  →  6. Toward Marriage
```

**There are two most common failure modes**: (1) moving too fast (heavy flirting / confessing / crossing lines before the stage is ready); (2) stalling in place (getting stuck in the Plateau and turning into a "good buddy / emotional dumping ground"). When diagnosing, locate the stage first, then judge whether he's "too eager" or "too timid."

Diagnostic method: judge the stage from the user's description (traits below), then point out the **core task of the current stage**, **where he's stuck**, and **the signs and actions for entering the next stage**. Combine this with that girl's profile (see profile-template.en.md) for a personalized read.

## Stage 0: Self-Development (the foundation running through everything)

This isn't a time period; it's something you work on forever. Whenever any stage gets stuck, the answer is often to come back here.
- **Hardware**: image (style/hair/physique/hygiene), income and career, lifestyle.
- **Software**: emotional stability, an interesting life history, social skills, a core that isn't starved for love.
- **Social base rate**: keep meeting new people; don't pin all your hopes on one person.
> If the user repeatedly agonizes over a single target and his neediness is off the charts, the root cause is usually too few options + low self-worth. Fix that first.

## Getting-to-Know Phase · Sub-stage 1: Attraction

- **Entry point (first contact / breaking the ice)**: just added each other on WeChat, just met by approaching her. Be the one to break the strangeness; open lightly (an emoji or a teasing line—no formal "hello," and no interrogating), and find topics from her situation/Moments.
- **Traits**: moving from "stranger/polite" to "able to actually chat," but you're still just "a guy who's okay."
- **Core task**: go from "okay" to "she starts to care a little"—convey emotion, demonstrate value, create curiosity.
- **Do**: push-pull, dual-track, cold reading, naturally surfacing your strengths and high-value sides, chatting catch-all topics, using mystery and pulling away at high points to keep you on her mind; demonstrate value indirectly (Moments + chat).
- **Common mistakes**: becoming a tool who answers every question on demand; information with no emotion; groveling to please; interrogating nonstop.
- **→ Signs of entering the next stage**: she reaches out first, starts topics on her own, responds to and enjoys your teasing, and shows clear interest signals.

## Getting-to-Know Phase · Sub-stage 2: Plateau (the most dangerous bottleneck)

- **Traits**: attraction has been established but the heat has cooled—chat becomes a daily logbook ("Have you eaten? / What are you up to? / Goodnight"), back-and-forth but no progress, the novelty fades, and there's a risk of **sliding back into ordinary friends**.
- **Why it's dangerous**: most people die here. They lack the nerve to move forward yet can't bear to let go, so the attraction they worked so hard to build gets ground away bit by bit through pointless, awkward small talk.
- **Core task**: **regenerate emotional swings + push the relationship toward flirtation**, breaking the downward curve of "plateau → too familiar → boring."
- **Do**:
  1. **Lower frequency and pull away**: stop messaging her every day, stop replying instantly. Deliberately leave gaps so she feels the contrast and thinks of you. "The busier you are, the more attractive you are."
  2. **Inject novelty and emotion**: switch up your topic approach—bring in push-pull, teasing, light flirtatious elements, and vivid, image-rich content to break the logbook.
  3. **Refresh her impression by demonstrating value indirectly**: use Moments to showcase your life and a high-energy state.
  4. **Decisively push it offline**: the best cure for the Plateau is meeting in person. Test the waters with a **soft/ambiguous invite** ("One of these days I'll take you to that XX place").
- **Common mistakes**: trying to "win back the heat" by chatting more / being more attentive (it backfires and exposes neediness); endlessly waiting to "ask her out once the vibe is right" (the vibe never comes).
- **→ Signs of entering the next stage**: she gives positive feedback to your push-pull and flirtation, accepts invites, and can meet in person.
- **If nothing warms her up no matter what you do**: attraction may have been insufficient to begin with, or her interest is fading—check for soft-rejection signals, cool things down / cut your losses if needed, and don't keep grinding.

## Getting-to-Know Phase · Sub-stage 3: Flirtation

- **Traits**: there are emotional swings, playful banter, tolerance for mild line-crossing, some sense of exclusivity she gives you, and a willingness to meet in person.
- **Core task**: raise the temperature + move the relationship offline and begin physical escalation.
- **Do**:
  1. Once the soft invite gets a response → use an **assumptive close** to lock in a specific time.
  2. Run the offline date per Section 5 of the knowledge base (location, venue changes, physical escalation).
  3. Apply moderate sexual tension, plant expectations, and elevate the relationship, calibrating intensity to her feedback.
- **Common mistakes**: never asking her out offline and dragging it into "good buddy" territory; or moving too fast and too hard and scaring her off; failing to handle shit tests.
- **→ Signs of entering the next stage**: meeting offline goes smoothly, she isn't resistant to physical contact, and she's clearly invested (planning a shared future, getting jealous, giving you exclusive time).

## Getting-to-Know Phase · Sub-stage 4: Relationship (making it official)

- **Principle**: **Confessing your feelings is "a confirmation of what has naturally come to be," not "a tactic to beg for a relationship."** When attraction and flirtation are both fully developed, making it official is almost a natural result.
- **Timing judgment**: she keeps giving interest signals, physical contact flows smoothly, she treats you as "someone special," and both sides tacitly assume you're heading toward being together—only then is making it official solid. If you're still chasing hard and gambling on a confession, it's most likely too early.
- **Method**: in many cases **sliding in naturally** (calling it after holding hands / kissing) beats a formal confession; a formal, ceremonial moment suits situations where it's already a near-certainty and you want to give her something definite.
- **Rejected/stalled**: if she's still clearly hesitant and avoidant at this point, it means earlier attraction was insufficient—don't keep pressing her with questions; fall back to building attraction or re-evaluate, and cut your losses if necessary.

## Stage 5: Long-Term

The lines and tactics exit; **the real substance takes the stage**:
- **Maintain emotional intensity**: don't slack off and revert to boring the moment you're official. Keep dating, creating experiences, and expressing appreciation.
- **Emotional resonance**: when she vents, she wants to be understood, not corrected. Empathize first, then address the issue.
- **Conflict handling**: lead with feelings ("I feel a bit hurt") before the problem; don't blame, dredge up old grievances, or stonewall. The silent treatment is a relationship killer.
- **Boundaries and respect**: neither grovel nor belittle—be equals, and keep your own space and social lives.
- **Assess long-term fit**: does the emotional support go both ways, is she willing to communicate during fights, and is she willing to fold you into her future and social circle.
- **Real-world progress**: meeting friends → meeting the parents → discussing future plans (city, buying a home, the pace toward marriage).

## Stage 6: Toward Marriage

- **The core is no longer attraction but compatibility and stewardship**: whether your worldviews, family backgrounds, money attitudes, living habits, and future plans actually fit.
- **Key matters**: meeting both sets of parents; candidly discussing real-world issues like bride price / buying a home / living apart / whether to have children; the ability to handle conflict together; sincerity about long-term commitment.
- **The proposal**: it's built on both sides having already confirmed they want to go forward; the ceremony is an expression, not persuasion.
- **Bottom-line reminder**: by this point, honesty, a sense of responsibility, and growing together matter far more than technique. If the relationship has to be sustained by "technique," the relationship itself may have a problem—helping the user see this clearly is also part of a coach's duty.

## Quick-Reference Diagnosis

| The user's situation | Most likely stage | First step to take |
|---|---|---|
| "Just got her WeChat, no idea what to talk about" | Attraction (first contact) | Break the ice lightly, find common ground, don't interrogate |
| "We chat fine but there's no spark / it feels like friends" | Attraction not yet stable, or already slid into Plateau | Add emotion and push-pull, create curiosity |
| "We used to chat with sparks flying; now it's daily awkward small talk / no topics" | **Plateau** | Lower frequency and pull away + inject novelty + decisively use a soft invite to push offline |
| "She's gone lukewarm about replying" | Plateau slide, or soft rejection | Check the signals: cool down and rein in neediness, or cut your losses |
| "I want to ask her out" | Flirtation | Soft invite → assumptive close; see Section 5 of the knowledge base |
| "We went on a date—what's next?" | Late Flirtation | Escalate physically, set up the next date, assess whether you can make it official |
| "I want to confess / make it official" | Pre-Relationship | First judge whether attraction is sufficient; if yes, confirm it naturally; if no, fall back to building attraction |
| "We're together but fight / go cold often" | Long-Term | Emotional maintenance + the conflict-communication method; return to Stage 5 |
| "Considering marriage but hesitant" | Toward Marriage | Talk real-world compatibility, not technique |
